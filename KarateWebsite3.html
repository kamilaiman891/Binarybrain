    GRADING

    Karate ranks and titles are used by most Karate styles, including Shotokan, Goju-Ryu, Wado-Ryu, Shito-Ryu, and Kyokushin. The "Black Belt" was designed as a way to show competence in the art, not mastery of it. Achieving 1st dan is the beginning of the journey, hence why kyus count down and then dans count up. The karate ranking system starts with no grade: white belt, though some schools use white as 10th kyu. The belt color order for Shito-Ryu Karate-Do is White Belt (9th Kyu), Yellow Belt (8th Kyu), Green Belt (7th Kyu), Blue Belt (6th Kyu), Purple Belt (5th Kyu), Brown Belt (1st - 4th Kyu) and Black (1st Dan - 10th Dan) . The black belt is the highest rank in Karate and represents the true expert having the highest rank After 9th Kyu, each grade will be expected to perform the current grading requirements and that of all previous grades. The colour belt grading is conducted approximately three times a year, and students who are deemed to have attained the required level of proficiency by the sensei are eligible to take the grading test. The grading test includes the demonstration of three bunkai for each kata, chosen by the candidate, and the etiquette and attitude of the candidate will also be taken into consideration. The Shito-Ryu syllabus stresses circular motion and shorter fighting methods, and it focuses on both hard and soft techniques. Shito-Ryu is one of the four main styles of karate and is extremely fast, but still can be artistic and powerful.
